Sight-seeing

Adventure abounds in Vermont! If you are looking to supplement your visit, we recommend some of the following activities:

Hiking

Vermont and Wisconsin share a lot of similarities, but Vermont has mountains! There is a list of trails in the area with varying degrees of difficulty, and they have a great set of trail recommendations based on your interests.

Bicycling

We are in the midst of several scenic bicycling routes. Cottonwood, the reception venue, is located directly adjacent to the Crown Point bridge across Lake Champlain between Vermont and New York. For lots of helpful tips and route suggestions in the area, please see Lake Champlain Bikeways.

Cheese!

There is on-going debate over the best cheese-producing state (Vermont or Wisconsin). If you would like to test out some of Vermont's finest, there is the VT Cheese Trail, which might take a few days, or you can visit a few local favorites:

  • The Middlebury Natural Foods Co-op sources local, artisan cheeses from around the state and is a favorite one-stop shop.
  • Dakin Farm: Cheeses, cured meats and always tons of samples early in the day. Make it a stop on a drive from Burlington. They usually have coupons for discounts on admission to Shelburne Farms.
  • Shelburne Farm: A great way to spend a day or half day with kids and adults of all ages. This is a working farm open for educational purposes with award-winning cheddar cheese and grounds designed by Frederick Law Olmsted. Visiting the childrens farm, walking the many trails on the property, or taking a wagon ride to the inn for a stroll in the lakeside gardens are all highly recommended!

Microbreweries

Ottercreek and Wolaver's Organic Ales are co-located in Middlebury. The tasting room also offers delicious sandwiches. To find Ottercreek and any of the area's other fine breweries, see: The Vermont Brewers Map.

Vineyards

We have stopped at Shelburne Vineyard near Shelburne Farm and Shelburne Museum. They offer mostly sweeter whites. If you are interested in exploring other vineyards, check out this map.

Farms and Fruit Picking

Some of Sarah's fondest memories of Vermont include afternoons spent fruit and berry picking. The available harvest over Labor Day weekend differs from year to year so it is always best to consult the farms listed under Addison County on Pick Your Own. We have had excellent experiences in the past at Champlain Orchards, Douglas Orchards, and Norris Berry Farm (with tons of fresh veggies, too!)

Meat

If you will be staying somewhere where you are able to prepare carnivorous meals for yourself, there are "honor system" meat lockers in the area that are also fun to explore. We have visited Duclos and Thompson Farm.

Golf

Sarah's family enjoys early morning golf at Basin Harbor Club.

Swimming

There are several state parks in our vicinity with swimming opportunities. Even at its warmest, Lake Champlain can be a little chilly for southerners. D.A.R. State Park, Button Bay State Park, and Kingsland Bay State Park are all recommended.

Dining

Bakeries and Tasty Treats

  • Gunnison Orchards Apples, apple cider donuts, fudge, cookies, and pies. The maple walnut cookies are a must! (518) 597-3363
  • Otter Creek Bakery A European-style bakery in Middlebury that offers espresso, breads, pastries, cookies, soups, salads, and sandwiches.
  • Vergennes Laundry A bakery and bistro in Vergennes offering croissants, pastries, sandwiches, salads, soups, and espresso. They will be closed for the month of September so be sure to fit in a visit before Monday!

Fine Dining

  • Black Sheep Bistro The name is an allusion to the patio seating available. French-inspired cuisine with a focus on local ingredients. Always a local favorite, but does require reservations.
  • Mary's at the Inn at Baldwin Creek A well-established VT tradition. They serve dishes for the season and source from their own farm, or those nearby. Reservations recommended.
  • Tourterelle French fusion cuisine, known for their crepes at brunch, but dinner is also highly recommended. Make reservations.

Getting Here

Air

The closest airport is in Burlington (BTV) at about 45 minutes - 1 hour away. Delta, JetBlue, United, and US Air all fly here.

We also consistently use Albany, NY (ALB), which is about 2 hours away, though traffic problems and winding mountain roads can sometimes lead to travel time up to 3 hours. Southwest offers direct flights from Orlando. Other airlines servicing the airport are Air Canada, Cape Air, Delta, United, and US Air.

Train

There is a train station directly across Lake Champlain (and easily accessible via the Crown Point bridge) in Port Henry, NY. Amtrak's Adirondack line services POH from New York's Penn Station or Montreal. The train has free wi-fi and follows Lake Champlain and the Hudson River along its whole length, making for a beautiful ride. Andy and I have used this 6-hour service and highly recommend it!

Bus

Middlebury and VT Transit offer service between Middlebury and Burlington and the airport for a very reasonable rate of $2 one-way via bus 116 or $4 one-way via CCTA Burlington LINK Monday through Friday. Alternatively, you can use Google Maps public transit option.

We also encourage carpooling or ride sharing with other guests and would be happy to help facilitate those arrangements if you are interested.
